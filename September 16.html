The Zhuangzi 莊子

Today we are going to be looking at the second major work in teh Daoist tradition. we will talk a lot about the stylistic differences between this and the laozi, note that htis is told mostly in fables.

Zhuangzi the person

This is the follow up to the Lao Zi. He was a contemporary of Mengzi, so the two of them represent the second generation of philosophers in those two tradtions, living about 100 years after confucius.

His given name was Zhou, and he was from "meng", though it is not exactly clear where that was (some think it was in modern day Henan, possibly in the state of Song). He lived during the 4th century BC, and what little we know about his life comes from the Records of the Grand Historian (recall that Laozi's informaitno was recorded in the same way). There is some biographical information in the book itself, but Burton Watson doubts its historical veracity. Watson speculates that if Zhuangzi were from the state of Song, he would have grown up in an environment which was commited to the rites from the Shang dynasty (as we can recall confucius was).

We also don't really know what connection the historical Zhuangzi has with the book istelf. Unlike the Laozi, it is assumed that the person who wrotes this was genearlly speaking the thinker behind the ideas as well (though its complicated textual history may obscrue this significantly).

Changes to the Text

Guo Xiang edited the text about 600 hundred years after it was first written. He was firmly of the opinion that many of the items in the book at his time had been added and were not authentic. He rearranged the chapters into the three sections I mentioned, and reduced the total number of chapters from 52 to 33.

Guo thought that only the first seciton, the inner chapters, were actualy by Zhuangzi. He thoguht the outer chapters were likely by like minded htinkers (modern scholars think the latter part of the book were indeed by other folks).

Daoists have conventionally treated the whole book as canon.

Themes

Watson says the central conciet of the Zhuangzi is "freedom". Recall the main focus of these early philsoophers was to bring order tothe insanity taht was happening around them.

Like laozi, the goal was to become free of the world.

One must discard conventional ideas.

Focused on spiritual side of things and a bit less on the political and intellectual elite.
